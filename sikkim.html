Discover Sikkim

A brief overview of Sikkim's geography, famous monuments, historical significance, language, culture, and food.

Geographical Features

Sikkim is characterized by its mountainous terrain, with elevations ranging from 280 meters to over 8,500 meters. The state is home to Kanchenjunga, the third highest peak in the world.

Famous Monuments

Notable monuments include the Rumtek Monastery, Pemayangtse Monastery, and the Namgyal Institute of Tibetology.

Historical Significance

Sikkim has a rich history that includes its establishment as a kingdom in the 17th century and its integration into India in 1975.

Language and Culture

Sikkim's official languages include Nepali, Sikkimese, and Lepcha. The state's culture is influenced by various ethnic groups, including the Lepchas, Bhutias, and Nepalese.

Food

Sikkimese cuisine includes dishes such as momos, gundruk, and thukpa. The food is known for its unique flavors and use of local ingredients.
